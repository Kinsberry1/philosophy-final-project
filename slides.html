Justice, Duty, and the Greater Good:
Ethical Conflicts in The Dark Knight and Captain America: Civil War

Keith Kingsberry

Introduction

Superhero narratives often explore deep moral conflicts. Heroes must choose between personal values and societal expectations.

Kantian Ethics (Duty-Based)

Morality is grounded in duty. Actions are judged based on intention, not outcomes.

Utilitarianism (Consequence-Based)

Ethical decisions should promote the greatest good for the greatest number of people.

Batman and Moral Duty

Batman adheres to strict moral codes. He refuses to kill, even if it would save lives in the short-term.

Captain America and Individual Rights

Captain America prioritizes personal freedom and moral responsibility over government mandates, even at great personal cost.

Conclusion

True heroism requires ethical sacrifice, balancing personal duty with the needs of society.
